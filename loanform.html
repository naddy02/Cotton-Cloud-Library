Interlibrary Loan Request Guidelines

Eligibility

  1. Who Can Request: ILL services are available to all registered patrons of the library. This includes students, faculty, staff, and community members with a valid library card.
  2. Account Status: Patrons must have a current library account in good standing (no overdue items or outstanding fines).

Requesting Items

  1. Types of Materials: Patrons can request books, journal articles, DVDs, and other media that are not available in the library's collection. Rare, fragile, or high-demand items may not be eligible for ILL.
  2. Submitting Requests: Requests can be submitted online through the library's ILL portal, in person at the reference desk, or via email.
  3. Information Needed: Provide complete and accurate information about the item, including title, author, publication date, ISBN/ISSN, and any other relevant details.

Processing and Delivery

  1. Processing Time: Most requests are processed within 2-3 business days. Delivery times vary depending on the lending library and the type of material requested.
  2. Notification: Patrons will be notified by email or phone when their requested items are available for pickup. Journal articles may be delivered electronically.
  3. Pickup Location: Physical items can be picked up at the main circulation desk unless otherwise specified.

Loan Periods and Renewals

  1. Loan Period: The loan period for ILL items is determined by the lending library and typically ranges from 2-4 weeks.
  2. Renewals: Renewal requests must be submitted before the due date. Renewals are granted at the discretion of the lending library and may not always be possible.
  3. Returns: ILL items must be returned to the circulation desk by the due date to avoid late fees.

Fees and Fines

  1. Cost: Most ILL services are free of charge. However, any fees imposed by the lending library will be passed on to the patron. Patrons will be informed of any potential fees before the request is processed.
  2. Overdue Fines: Overdue ILL items are subject to fines as determined by the lending library. These fines will be added to the patron's account.
  3. Lost or Damaged Items: Patrons are responsible for any lost or damaged ILL items. Replacement costs and processing fees will be charged according to the lending library's policies.

Restrictions and Limitations

  1. Maximum Requests: Patrons may have a maximum of [specific number] ILL requests at any one time.
  2. Textbooks: Requests for current textbooks may be restricted or not processed, depending on library policy.
  3. High-Demand Items: Some newly released or high-demand items may not be available through ILL.

Contact Information

  1. Assistance: For help with ILL requests, patrons can contact the ILL department via phone, email, or in person during library hours.
  2. Feedback: Patrons are encouraged to provide feedback on their ILL experience to help improve services.
